Bricks of Us

You bring a brick, I bring one too

Each shaped by storms that we've been through.

Some are smooth, some chipped and worn,

Stories carved where hearts were torn.

From separate walls, we take them down,

No need for thrones, no need for crowns.

We stack them high, side by side,

A place where love and trust reside.

Not just mine, not only yours,

But something new, with open doors.

A home that stands through wind and rain,

Built with hands that heal, not strain.

So bring your brick, I'll bring mine too,

And we will build-both old and new.
